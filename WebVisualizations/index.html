Summary: MLB Pitchers vs HoF

I'm a huge baseball fan, and my favorite team is the Chicago White Sox. My favorite player to watch over the last 20 years was Mark Buehrle. I'm interested in seeing how his career stats compare to other pitchers all-time. How does he compare to Hall of Fame pitchers in terms of wins above replacement (WAR) and earned run average (ERA)? That's the question I'm looking to answer with my analysis, and along the way I'll also be able to gain other insights about other players.

Key Questions:

  1. Is Mark Buehrle a hall of famer?
  2. Which retired players,not already in HoF, stack up best against HoF players all-time?
  3. Which active players are likely on the path to the HoF?
  4. Which HoF players appear to not belong based on these metrics?

Summary of findings:

Does Mark Buehrle belong in the hall of fame? The data alone for WAR and ERA suggests he's likely going to come up short of reaching the HoF, but definitely the hall of very good. His career totals for WAR and ERA are both below the HoF average. Although he doesn't quite have the stats, his career accolades could get him some consideration.

  • His career spanned 16 seasons from 2000-2015.
  • His number, 56, has been retired by the White Sox
  • 214 wins in 493 career starts
  • 5 All-Star appearances and 4 Gold Gloves
  • 1 perfect game vs Tampa Bay on 7/23/2009 and 1 no-hitter vs Texas on 4/18/2007
  • World Series champion in 2005
  • He led the American League in innings pitched in both the 2004 and 2005 seasons
  • Went 49 consecutive starts pitching at least 6 innings
  • In 2009, he had a streak of 45 consectuive batters retired
  • 14 straight seasons with at least 200 innings pitched
